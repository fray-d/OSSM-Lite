3. Set motor constraints

If you're using the standard 57AIM30 "gold" motor you can ignore this section and move on to the next step. Otherise you might need to configure your motor constraints so the firmware can calculate distances and speeds accurately.

Click the connect button and fill the form with settings that match your hardware. A chart is provided below with the settings for some motors. If yours is not in the list look it up from the manufacturer, and consider sending me a note with the details so I can update the chart.

You can also use this area to set a global speed limit by setting the motor RPM and/or max acceleration to lower values. Just because your motor can do 3000 RPM doesn't mean you want it to do so. If you find your device doesn't have enough low-end speed and tops out higher then you would like feel free to reduce the RPM.

Most motors should be programmed to 800 steps per revolution. They often don't come this way from the factory. This guide does not provide instructions or tools for motor flashing at this time. You can try using more then the recommended value, but the ESP32 can only send so many pulses per second before you reach it's limit. This is why 800 was choosen.

You can also modify the number of teeth on your pulley and the pitch of your belt. Technically even if you have changed these parts from the default you are not required to update these values. The machine will still work, it just will not measure the rail accurately. This could be a problem if you use a manual or semi-manual homing method.

Motor Max RPM Steps per revolution
57AIM30 1500 800
60AIM40 1500 800
IHSV 3500 800 or 2000

Requirements: Chrome, Edge, or Opera on desktop (not mobile).
